Easy peasy, you only have to...

  1. Prepare data file(s). Make a transformation of your data from whatever format you keep it in to one of the Linked Places format "flavors" (details below)
  2. Upload data file(s). Your uploaded file(s) are checked for valid format. If valid, immediately proceed to Step 3.
  3. Insert to WHG database. Your data is maintained in a set of read-only tables [model documentation], which are the basis for subsequent steps. Screens for (a) managing the contribution and (b) viewing the tabular data and map (if applicable) are now available.
  4. Reconciliation (modern authorities). The automated step of finding matches between your place records and records in modern name authority resources (Getty TGN, DBpedia, Geonames, and Wikidata) with our reconciliation tool.
  5. Review reconciliation results. Potential matches returned by Step 4 must be reviewed manually. Our review tool queues them up for you and your team to decide whether a hit is a close match, exact match, or otherwise related. Each positive response augments your dataset by adding a record in the place_link table. You can also choose to augment your record with geometries from matches, were they exist, generating a new place_geom record.
  6. Reconciliation (WHGazetteer). Now it's time to see whether the places your records reference already exist in WHGazetteer. Steps 4 and 5 are essentially repeated, this time against our union index.
  7. Review reconciliation results. If a contributor's record shares a modern authority link with a record in our index, it is considered to be a match, and automatically becomes a "child" attestation within that index record. The potential matches suggested on the basis of semantics (e.g. shared name, type, containing polity) are reviewed as in Step 5.
  8. Submit for accessioning. Your contribution is now ready. Clicking "submit" will flag it for a final check by the WHGazetteer team. If it all looks okay, we'll add your data -- a combination of child records and new 'parent' records -- to the union index, and your data will appear in the graphical interface and in API queries. Woo hoo!

Contribution Format(s)

Two data formats that can be used for WHGazetteer contributions: (a) Linked Places format: JSON-LD and GeoJSON-T compatible, allowing for detailed sourcing of names and geometries, and temporal scoping of names, geometries, place types, and relations; and (b) an abbreviated CSV form appropriate for simpler records.
